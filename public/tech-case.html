Overview

What It Is

Student teams solve real-world problems by developing innovative, technology-driven solutions across key tracks, competing for awards, with finalists presenting on event day.

It goes beyond a traditional business case competition. It challenges students to integrate technology into their own field to solve real-world problems. No prototype is required; teams present a clear, structured, and feasible solution that shows how a problem can be solved using technology.

Guidelines & requirements

  • Open to all disciplines; students are encouraged to pick a track aligned with their field of study.
  • Does not require a prototype; the focus is problem understanding, innovation, feasibility, and impact.
  • Minimum of two participants per team.
  • Submissions must be completed before event day.
  • Finalists present using a slide presentation during the event.

Judging rubric

Five criteria, each weighted 20% · scored 1 (Poor) – 5 (Average) – 10 (Excellent).

20%

Problem Understanding & Relevance

Clearly defines the real-world problem, its context, and significance, strongly aligned with the selected track.

20%

Solution Design & Innovation

A creative, feasible, well-structured solution that leverages technology effectively.

20%

Technical Application & Feasibility

A clear implementation approach that demonstrates feasibility and scalability.

20%

Communication & Presentation

Clear, structured, engaging delivery that simplifies complex ideas.

20%

Impact, Value & Scalability

Strong potential real-world impact; scalable, practical, and valuable.
