Overview

What It Is

A Research & Project Showcase. Participants compete for awards and recognition across two categories, Research and Projects, spanning all domains.

Both apply technology, but they differ in structure and expected outcomes. Morgan TechFest does not publish submissions; participants may present both previously published and unpublished work.

Research

Research presentations focus on investigation, analysis, and findings, following a structured academic format: problem statement or research objective, methodology, results or findings, and discussion and recommendations. Research may be theoretical or applied, but must demonstrate clear analysis, depth of study, and a contribution to knowledge.

Research judging · scored 1 (Poor) – 5 (Average) – 10 (Excellent).

1–10

Overall Impact

Conveys the motivations, context, conclusions, and broader impact of the research.

1–10

Appearance & Organization

Well-organized, clear, and visually effective; design and layout aid understanding.

1–10

Communication & Presentation

Explains the topic, methods, findings, and implications for a non-specialist audience.

1–10

Technical Knowledge

Situates the research within its field and shows mastery of related work.

1–10

Oral Presentation & Answering Questions

Organized, confident delivery; competent and expert when answering questions.

Projects

Project presentations focus on building, implementation, and practical solutions. Submissions must include a clearly defined problem, a developed solution (prototype, system, or product), and demonstrable results or functionality. Projects may originate from coursework, competitions, or independent work, but must be fully developed and functional at the time of submission.

Project judging · five criteria, each weighted 20%.

20%

Overall Impact

Showcases the project’s importance and impact on the field and society.

20%

Appearance & Organization

Visually appealing, well-organized, and easy to follow.

20%

Communicates clearly, simplifying complex ideas for a general audience.

20%

Technical Knowledge

Demonstrates strong technical expertise and deep understanding of the project.

20%

Oral Presentation & Answering Questions

Confident, well-prepared, and handles questions with expertise.
